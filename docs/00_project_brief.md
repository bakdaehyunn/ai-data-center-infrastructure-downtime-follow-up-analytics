# Project Brief

## Focus

Maintenance Downtime Follow-up Analytics answers one operational question:

> Which maintenance requests are delaying equipment return-to-service, where is the blocker, and what should the team follow up next?

## Need

Maintenance downtime follow-up often depends on evidence scattered across request records, workflow events, work orders, parts availability, inspections, sensor alerts, equipment, and production line context.

That fragmentation creates a practical blind spot. A team may know that work is open, but not whether the real delay is review, assignment, parts waiting, active repair, inspection, repeat failure, or line impact.

This project turns those scattered records into a decision-support layer: current state, delay reason, data trust, and ranked next follow-up.

## Users

- Maintenance supervisors who need a daily follow-up queue
- Maintenance planners who need to unblock parts, assignments, and inspections
- Reliability engineers who need repeat-failure and asset-delay signals
- Operations managers who need to understand production line impact

## Decision Output

The output is a ranked follow-up queue with:

- delayed request
- current blocker stage
- equipment and line impact
- parts risk
- repeat failure signal
- data quality flags
- recommended next action

## Not In Scope

- Transaction entry or work order editing
- Inventory management
- Crew scheduling optimization
- Comparing unrelated business domains
- Replacing an existing maintenance system of record
